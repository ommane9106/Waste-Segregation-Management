How to Segregate Waste

Proper segregation of waste helps in effective recycling and disposal. Hereâ€™s a simple guide to categorize your household waste:

Dry Waste

This includes paper, plastic, glass, metal, and textiles. Make sure to clean and dry these items before disposing of them.

  • Newspapers, magazines, cardboard
  • Plastic bottles, containers, wrappers
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Metal cans and foil
  • Old clothes and shoes

Wet Waste

This category includes biodegradable waste such as kitchen scraps and garden waste. Wet waste is ideal for composting.

  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Leftover food
  • Tea bags, coffee grounds
  • Grass clippings, leaves, flowers

Hazardous Waste

Waste that can be harmful to health or the environment must be disposed of properly at designated collection points.

  • Batteries and cell phones
  • Paints, solvents, chemicals
  • Expired medicines
  • Electronic waste (e-waste)
  • Light bulbs and fluorescent tubes
