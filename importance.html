Why Waste Segregation Matters

Proper waste segregation plays a vital role in protecting the environment, conserving resources, and improving public health. By dividing waste into categories such as dry, wet, and hazardous, we can reduce landfill overflow, promote recycling, and minimize pollution.

Protects Environment

Segregating waste helps reduce pollution by preventing hazardous substances from contaminating soil, water, and air.

Encourages Recycling

Sorted waste is easier to recycle, helping conserve raw materials and reduce energy consumption in production.

Improves Public Health

Proper segregation prevents the spread of diseases caused by pests and reduces health hazards from toxic waste.

Reduces Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Decomposing organic waste in landfills emits methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Segregation helps composting and reduces these emissions.

Conserves Natural Resources

By recycling materials like paper, glass, and metal, we save trees, minerals, and energy, protecting natural ecosystems.
